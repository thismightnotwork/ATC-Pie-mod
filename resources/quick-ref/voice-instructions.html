Voice instructions

Supported instructions

All instructions normally available through the panel or mouse gestures when voice recognition is turned off can be recognised through speech with the appropriate phraseology, except:

  • departure clearances;
  • ground taxi instructions;
  • complex navigation instructions such as "intercept the 120° radial from ..." or "proceed ..." with a full route specification.
For those, you must use the mouse interface.

Examples

The following examples are recognised by the system:

  • "Speedbird fifteen seventy-two turn left heading three two zero"
  • "Koreanair one niner tree seven descend and maintain one one eleven thousand"
  • "November nine one Lima proceed direct Mike Lima Alpha expect runway zero six left"
  • "Foxtrot Uniform Tango cleared visual approach runway one three"
  • "Springbok two zero five runway three cleared to land wind three two zero at six knots"

Tips to maximise accuracy

  • Do not break, backtrack or silence too much between your words. Try to have your message flow from start to end.
  • Take special care enunciating the callsign. Abbreviated tail numbers for non-commercial flights are OK as long as at they end with at least two of the last letters or numbers.
  • The grammar allows for some laxity in the phraseology, but the more accurate your instructions, the safer your results.
  • Do not set your microphone volume too low.

Instruction grammar

Below is a grammar for a relevant subset of the recognised instruction language, in EBNF style. The actual grammar file is resources/speech-recog/instr.jsgf, in JSGF format.

FULL_MESSAGE ::= callsign instruction+

instruction ::=
| "turn" ["left" | "right"] "heading" heading
| ("climb" | "descend") ["and maintain"] (FL | altitude)
| "speed" (speed | "your discretion")
| "expect" [approach] (helipad | runway)
| "intercept localiser" [runway]
| "cleared" ("ILS" | "visual") "approach" [runway]
| "make straight-in approach" [helipad | runway]
| runway "line-up and wait"
| runway "cleared for take-off" [wind_info]
| runway "cleared to land" [wind_info]
| "cancel approach" | "go around"
| "proceed direct" navpoint
| "hold at" navpoint
| "squawk" xpdr_code
| "contact" atc_position ["bye bye"]
| "say intentions"

approach ::= "ILS" | "visual" | "straight-in"
atc_position ::= "ramp" | "ground" | "tower" | "departure" | "approach" | "centre"

callsign is either a tail number or a commercial flight number using the airline callsign specified in CONFIG/phon/airline-callsigns.phon.

navpoint can be spelt with the aeronautical alphabet or named as specified for the location; see CONFIG/phon/Notice.
